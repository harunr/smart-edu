About the LMIP Research Programme

The Research Programme on Labour Market Intelligence (LMI) is a five-year research programme initiated in 2019 by the Department of Higher Education and Training (DHET) with support from the National Skills Fund (NSF).

The programme aims to contribute in a systematic and focussed manner to the establishment of a credible framework for skills planning in South Africa.

Search  LMIP PHASE 1 Publications
Goal

The LMI has a broad scope which brings together various research projects that will identify current and potential future skills shortages as well as assess the relationship between education and the economy from an
inter-disciplinary perspective.

Our goal is to produce new research on a variety of important and interconnected issues that cover labour market analysis, identifying skills shortages and required interventions, examining the relationships between qualifications and occupational classifications at the individual and institutional level, and recognising skills requirements for sustainable livelihoods in communities.

The research programme will contribute to the development of a system of monitoring and reporting on post-school education and training institutions, as well as building capacity on labour market intelligence through a variety of training programmes and workshops.

The different parts of the education system should work together allowing learners to take different pathways that offer high quality learning opportunities. There should be clear linkages between schools, FET colleges, universities of technology, universities and other providers of education and training. There should also be clear linkages between education and training and the world of work

- National Development Plan 2030, p.296
